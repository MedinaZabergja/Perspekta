Perspective Pivot – Project Proposal
A Cozy Cognitive Reframing Tool for Healthier Thought Reflection

1. Introduction
Perspective Pivot is a digital self‑reflection tool designed to help users examine and
reframe difficult thoughts using principles inspired by Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
(CBT). The application aims to guide users through a structured process of recognizing
a distressing thought, evaluating the evidence surrounding it, and forming a more
balanced perspective.
The goal of the project is not to replace therapy or professional mental‑health support,
but rather to provide a gentle reflective tool that encourages mindful thinking and healthy
emotional distance from rumination.

2. Problem Statement
Many people experience intrusive or distressing thoughts during their daily lives. When
individuals repeatedly focus on these thoughts without reflection, it can lead to
rumination loops that worsen emotional distress. Existing journaling or mental‑health
applications often encourage constant engagement, which may unintentionally reinforce
repetitive thinking.
Perspective Pivot attempts to address this issue by guiding users through a brief
cognitive reframing exercise and then encouraging them to step away from the screen
and engage in real‑world activities.

3. Project Objectives
The main objectives of the application are:
 Help users identify and examine distressing thoughts.
 Introduce simple cognitive reframing techniques inspired by CBT.
 Reduce rumination by limiting repeated engagement with the same thought.
 Encourage healthy offline activities after reflection.
 Provide a calm, comforting user interface that promotes emotional safety.

4. Core Concept: The Perspective Pivot Exercise
The core interaction within the application is the &#39;Perspective Pivot&#39; exercise. The user
begins by writing a distressing thought. The app then guides them through a structured
challenge process where they search for evidence that questions or balances the
thought.
Rather than forcing positive thinking, the tool encourages users to explore alternative
perspectives and arrive at a more balanced interpretation of the situation.

5. User Interface and Emotional Design
The user interface will prioritize comfort and calmness through a soft, warm design style.
Rounded elements, gentle animations, and pastel color palettes will create a welcoming
environment. A small mascot character may act as a visual guide throughout the
reflection process, helping the experience feel supportive rather than clinical.
A visual &#39;lens&#39; metaphor will represent cognitive clarity. When a user first enters a
negative thought, the screen appears slightly blurred or darkened. As the user adds
evidence and completes the reflection steps, the visual filter gradually becomes brighter
and clearer, symbolizing a shift in perspective.

6. Reflection Mode and Rumination Prevention
To prevent users from becoming stuck in repetitive reflection cycles, the application
introduces a temporary pause system after an entry is completed. During this time, the
user is encouraged to step away from the app and focus on real‑world activities.
Instead of immediately allowing new entries, the app enters &#39;Reflection Mode&#39;. In this
state, the user receives gentle reminders that thoughts may still appear throughout the
day, but they have already taken a meaningful step by reflecting on one thought.

7. Offline Activity Suggestions
To encourage healthy behavioral activation, the application provides small offline activity
suggestions when Reflection Mode is active. These activities aim to shift the user&#39;s
attention away from the screen and toward creative or relaxing actions.
 Drawing something playful such as a silly cat.
 Writing three positive observations about the day.
 Taking a short walk or stretching for a few minutes.
 Making simple crafts such as folding paper stars.

 Preparing a warm drink and focusing on sensory experiences.

8. Ethical and Safety Considerations
Because the application deals with emotional reflection, it must include clear safety
guidelines. During onboarding, users will see a message explaining that the tool is
intended for self‑reflection and does not replace therapy or professional mental‑health
care.
If a user enters phrases that suggest severe distress or self‑harm, the application should
gently encourage them to reach out to trusted individuals or professional support
services. The system will not attempt to diagnose mental‑health conditions or provide
clinical advice.

9. Thought History and Reflection
To reduce the risk of triggering rumination, previously entered thoughts will not be
immediately visible. Instead, entries may become available after a longer reflection
period (for example, 30 days). When reviewing past thoughts, the user will be prompted
with reflective questions such as how the thought feels today compared to when it was
originally written.

10. Proposed Technology Stack
 Frontend: React or Next.js
 Styling: Tailwind CSS
 Database and authentication: Supabase (free tier)
 Server-side functions: Next.js serverless functions

11. Conclusion
Perspective Pivot aims to combine thoughtful psychological principles with
compassionate design. By focusing on structured reflection, rumination prevention, and
offline engagement, the application seeks to create a healthy and ethical digital
environment for self‑reflection.
The project demonstrates how technology can support emotional awareness while
maintaining clear boundaries around professional mental‑health care.
